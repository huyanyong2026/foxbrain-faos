# VAFOX Mobile Home Experience

Version: Genesis-Construction-002  
Product: VAFOX Outdoor LIFE  
Principle: mobile primary, desktop secondary.

## 1. Mobile-First Rule

Every Home must be designed first for the phone. Desktop may provide wider layout and deeper review, but it must not become the primary mental model.

When a user opens VAFOX on a phone, they should know within 3 seconds:

1. Who am I?
2. What matters today?
3. What should I do?
4. How does AI help?

## 2. First Screen Contract

Every Home first screen must contain:

- Identity: VID-backed person, active role, relationship context.
- Today: one to three prioritized enterprise or personal signals.
- Action: the next mission, decision, or learning step.
- AI: one natural-language entry point.

First screen must not contain:

- Dense dashboards.
- Data source selectors.
- Agent selectors.
- ERP-style menu trees.
- Unprioritized report lists.

## 3. Mobile Navigation Pattern

Recommended navigation for Home experiences:

- Home.
- Today.
- Missions or Decisions.
- Ask AI / Ask VAFOX.
- Me.

Navigation labels may adapt by Home type, but the underlying interaction model should remain consistent.

## 4. Interaction Rhythm

```text
Open phone
  ↓
Recognize identity
  ↓
Understand today
  ↓
Act on mission or decision
  ↓
Ask AI if needed
  ↓
Learn and grow
```

## 5. Content Hierarchy

Mobile content priority:

1. Critical alert or risk.
2. Required mission or decision.
3. Today summary.
4. AI suggestion.
5. Supporting evidence.
6. Deeper details.

Details must be progressive. The Home should feel like a living assistant, not a database browser.

## 6. Desktop Secondary Role

Desktop supports:

- Longer reading.
- Multi-column evidence review.
- Complex approval review.
- Administrative configuration by authorized users.
- Implementation and operations work.

Desktop must not introduce a separate product concept from mobile Home.

## 7. Accessibility and Reliability

Mobile Home should support:

- Readable typography.
- Clear touch targets.
- Low-bandwidth graceful loading.
- Freshness indicators for critical data.
- Offline or degraded-state messaging when Core context is unavailable.
- Secure session recovery through Gateway.
